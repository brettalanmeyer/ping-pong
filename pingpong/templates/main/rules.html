Rules

Serving and General Rules

  • If you throw the ball in the air, attempt to hit it, and fail, it is a loss of point.
  • ANY serve (including a net hit) that does not hit your side first, followed by you opponents side is a loss of point.
  • All serves must be placed open hand and tossed vertically aproximately 6 inches behind the edge of the table.
  • Only the table top is a valid surface. Sides of the table are the same as missing the table.

Singles

  • A match is played best of 1, 2 of 3, or 3 of 5 games.
  • For each game, the first player to reach 21 points (or otherwise specified) wins that game, however a game must be won by at least a two point margin.
  • The player in the 'green' corner will be first to serve.
  • Serving will switch every 5 serves until game point.
  • For game point, the player that does not have game point will serve.
  • If game point goes away, serving reverts back to every 5th serve.

Doubles

  • A match is played best of 1, 2 of 3, or 3 of 5 games.
  • For each game, the first player to reach 21 points (or otherwise specified) wins that game, however a game must be won by at least a two point margin.
  • The player in the 'green' corner will be first to serve.
  • Serving will switch every 5 serves until game point.
  • ALL serves must be hit from serving team's right square first, followed by the returning teams diagonal square. Any serve that fails to meet this condition (even a net serve) results in loss of point.
  • Immediately after serving, the player switches position with their teammate.
  • You cannot lose on a serve.

Nines

  • Each player starts with 9 points (or otherwise specified).
  • Before the match can begin, someone must declare "nines" and will be the first to serve.
  • If the ball touches your corner of the table, it is your responsibility to return the ball.
  • If the returner misses the ball or is hit off of the table, they lose a point.
  • If a player loses a point and only has one point remaining, they are granted a single courtesy serve.
  • Once a player has run out of points, they are out of the game.
  • The last player with points remaining is the winner.
  • In the event that both players on one side of the table are out, the app will determine where the remaining players will be.
